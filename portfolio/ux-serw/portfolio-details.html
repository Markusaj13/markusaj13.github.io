SERW - Connecting the elderly and reducing loneliness

About this project

SERW is a newly founded company who aim to reduce loneliness among the elderly by offering a variety of free events like lunches or museum visits.
However during 2020, as Covid-19 hit the world, Serw needed a new way to get the elderly to interact more with the website as social events were heavily restricted.
Patrik Larsson, founder of serw.se, reached out to our school in search of a capable UX-team to help them move their company in the right direction.
After a quick review of their website, especially the mobile version, my team decided that we wanted to help as we felt we could really make a change for this company.

The goal

The key goal Was to design a user-friendly website alternative based on user research
Our job would be to redesign the website to make it more accessible as the company now needed to rely more heavily on web-based events and activities than before. In addition to this, our team took it upon ourselves to create our own, separate goal. We created a completely new service for the website along with the new design, to give the company a better idea of what they needed to focus on design wise in the future. The new service was Forums.
Based on previous research by the company, most users were using mobile devices to browse serw.se so we chose to design our project using “mobile first” thinking.

The challenge

The biggest challenge was making the website design as accessible as possible to the target audience, people over the age of 65, and taking into account the extensive amount of criteria needed to fulfill WCAG standards.
Another great challenge of ours was reaching out to the target audience during Covid-19 to conduct surveys, interviews, and usability tests. We instead had to rely on digital surveys and testing which eventually proved reliable as well.

The process and solution

User research – Understanding the user
To understand the user, we created a survey to define the target audience’s digital habits. We felt that a survey by e-mail was the best way to reach a large amount of people, especially in the time of Covid-19. We then compiled and presented the results to the customer who based on the answers had follow up questions he wanted answered. We iterated and created a new survey from which we got a lot more information
After a lot of data collection, however helpless the elderly first seemed, research showed that a larger percent of the people we surveyed and interviewed were a lot more tech-savvy than we first anticipated as many of them were active on social platforms like Instagram and Facebook. However their willingness to try new things on the internet were not as high as we would have liked.

Empathize/Define/Ideate
From these interviews we were able to create an accurate persona and a believable problem statement which we could use as a solid starting point for our project. We also created mood boards to help us get the feeling of the direction we were headed, an effect-map to get a clear idea of what steps we needed to take to achieve our goal, a mindmap, derived from a board of post-it notes, of our ideas for the best user experience of the website and a Sitemaps to give ourselves a structured blueprint from which to start building the website!

Prototyping
At this stage we started sketching and later testing our lo-fi wireframes. We tested and compared the results to all our documentations and proceeded to iterate until we felt the lo-fi wireframes were ready to be upgraded to a hi-fi prototype. We preformed usability tests and iterated the prototype until we reached our final product!

One of my strengths being my sense of detail and organizing, I took on the responsibility of structuring our work as the project went on. Another of my strengths during the project was the prototyping tool Figma. Because of this I took on the roll as the main organizer, in charge of maintaining and coordinating the prototype.
My experience from a previous project and ability to learn quickly gave me the skills and to teach my fellow classmates more about ‘Figma’, which is something I love to do.
I love to learn new things, but if I can teach one person one thing, especially something I am passionate about, that has been a good day!

The results

We created better, more complete phone adapted webpage design with high accessibility and a sleek design.
We received a very satisfied customer who will use our new template as a guide to develop the rest of the website.

Lessons learned

Research: This was the first time I focused so heavily on user research in a project, and I learned a lot about how valuable user research is on the path to understanding the users needs and desires!
Live case: This was my first real world project where I actually had a customer relying on me and my team. This one of the greatest learning experiences!
Connect: I realized that I am not bothering the customer by asking questions. The customer wants to be involved and is often happy to give inputs. Don’t be afraid to reach out to the customer!
Kill your darlings: Sometimes you must accept that you need to drop parts of your idea and compromise! Try combining it with your teammates idea instead and a third teammate might se the hidden gold nugget within your combined efforts!
Team work: Take advantage of the team and each individuals strengths and learn to discuss with my team what is important to focus on at the moment and what is not.
