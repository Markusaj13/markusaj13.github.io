Förgätmigej - Förskoleappen

About this project

With this app, Förgätmigej, we wanted to simplify contact and the stream of information between pre-schools and parents.

The goal

To create a prototype of an app where parents could have easy access to all information regarding their child and the children’s education whilst making the app sleek, minimalistic and with a design that inspires calm rather than stress in a parents already stressed out life.

The challenge

To make the app preferable to old methods and competing apps. We wanted to show users a good reason to switch to our product by keeping it usable and reliable with minimal explanation or guidance needed.

The process and solution

User research
We started off with a target group analysis by conducting interviews with parents who had children in pre-school.

Empathize/Define/Ideate
From these interviews we were able to create a persona of the user. We also created Mood boards, Sitemaps, Problem statements and more to really understand the user and their needs!

Prototyping
At this stage we really needed to consider accessibility. We used WCAG-standards to make our app as accessible as possible for the people in, as well as people outside of, the target audience. We then created lo-fi wireframe sketches for a possible layout and executed usability tests, after which we iterated to the point where we could start adding design which we also iterated until we were satisfied with the project!

My Contribution

Although we all worked on every part and step of the project, naturally we gravitate towards our strengths. Me and one other student very organically took on the leader role and were the driving forces of the project.
One of my strengths being my sense of detail and organizing, I took on the responsibility of structuring our work as the project went on, which proved most useful for the final presentation of the project.
Another thing that came naturally to me was the prototyping software ‘Figma’. I really fell in love with it and learned very quickly how to maintain and coordinate the prototype.
Me being a fast learner gave me the skills and ability to teach my fellow classmates more about ‘Figma’, which is something I love to do.
I love to learn new things, but if I can teach one person one thing, that has been a good day!

The results and lessons learned

We created a prototype with a minimalistic and sleek design along with a comprehensive chart of UI-elements for the developer. For a first project myself, and the team are very satisfied with the outcome. As this was my first UX-project I definitely learned a lot! But I must say that the greatest learning experience was to create something like this step-by-step in a team of other UX-designers. I learned how to better cooperate with teammates who both shared my opinions and with those who had opinions that differed from mine.
